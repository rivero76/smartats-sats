## 2) Strategic Assessment (From Technical Audit)

1. Current strength: advanced trust-first ATS matcher with evidence-grounded AI outputs.
2. Current gap: still mostly "bring your own job" workflow; 60% threshold is not yet an automated trigger.
3. Current onboarding gap: LinkedIn ingestion backend (Stories 1-2) is implemented; HITL Review UI (Story 3) is the remaining delivery gap before the full flow is release-ready.
4. Strategic direction: preserve trust layer and add proactive orchestration in the next sprint sequence.
5. **Emerging competitive gap (2026):** AI SaaS without BYOK or model selection is increasingly a friction point for enterprise buyers, privacy-sensitive users, and markets with data-residency requirements. BYOK is now a baseline expectation, not a differentiator. P17 closes this gap and directly unlocks new market segments without requiring a pricing redesign.
6. **Commercial readiness gap (2026-03-30 CEO review):** The product is technically sophisticated but pre-commercial. Three hard blockers exist before any revenue is possible: (a) no payment infrastructure — no Stripe, no subscription management, no billing portal; (b) feature gating is schema-only — `sats_tenant_features` exists but enforcement is not wired, meaning every user accesses every feature at zero cost today; (c) no self-service onboarding — no pricing page, no upgrade CTA, no tier awareness for users. Estimated path to soft launch: 6 weeks. Priority sequence: validate open blockers → P22 billing → P23 gating → P24 onboarding → launch.
7. **Differentiation narrative for marketing:** The Career Aspirations Tracker (P-CAT) is the strongest unique value proposition for marketing — "SmartATS doesn't just score your resume, it shows you what the next 5 years of your career should look like." This story is worth waiting one additional sprint to deliver before going to market.
